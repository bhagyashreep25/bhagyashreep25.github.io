BUSINESS BUDDY
Simplifying digital payments for minority-owned businesses

To challenge myself and test my critical thinking skills, I took part in a UX design makeathon. I decided to take on the challenge sponsored by Visa, aimed at helping minority-owned small business owners setup digital payments.

RESEARCH
Literature Review

I performed literature review to better understand the target users, small businesses, digital payments and how the three coalesce together.

These are the key themes that emerged from the literature review:

Infrastructure is the first hurdle to setup digital payments.

Minority-owned businesses have a hard time sustaining as compared to non-minority owned.
The business owner’s perception of digital payment’s usefulness for the business, case of use, and trust towards it directly plays part in their attitude towards it.
Lower costs, increased control, increased incentive to save, increased risk management are some benefits to recipients of digital payments.




CONCEPTION
Coming up with a four-tiered solution

After performing literature review, I used the design thinking approach to come up with a set of "How Might We" questions and used them to ideate the solutions.

I came up with these targeted solutions to support the minority business owners:

Community

Leveraging tightly-knit minority communities
Blog

Read from experts about getting what you need and security 101
Step-by-step breakdown

Follow through easy steps to easily transition to cashless payments
Store

Buy what you need
