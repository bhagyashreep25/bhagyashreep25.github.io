PROCESS
Background Research


TYPES OF MOOD TRACKERS

Two types: Survey-based & Chatbot-based

However, they lack in-depth tracking, and reflecting capabilities.
AUTOMATED MOOD SENSING

It involves using data from sensors embedded in smartwatches such as heart rate biosignals, sleep and activity trackers.

It drastically reduces user input overhead.
MULTI-MODAL TRACKING, REFLECTING ON DATA

The importance of engaging with and reflecting on self-tracking data and the complexity of behavior change was discovered.

Papers also put forth the merits of multi-modal tracking, natural language feedback and contextual information.




PROCESS
Target Audience & Personas

The target users of PET can be characterized in the following ways:
1. Those individuals who seek to track their emotional states over time
2. Those individuals who may (or may not) have inconsistent abilities to interact with a mood tracking app
3. Those individuals who seek an outlet to express their emotions or find companionship on an emotional basis
4. Those individuals who seek to customize or personalize their mood tracking experience

EFFICIENT CUSTOMIZATION & REFLECTIONS

Avatar Customization (change the avatar, customize color palette)

Mood trends (time-based, linked to contextual data)

Easy-to-follow visualizations (bar charts, time series-graphs) to promote reflection
PERSONAL CONTEXT INTEGRATION

Automatic population of activity and event data from the iOS calendar app or Google Calendar

PROCESS
Iterating on the wireframes

I conducted a round of testing with my classmates to check for intuitiveness and presentation of the various aspects of the app. I discovered some important points to address which are stated below:

1. It wasn't clear that the home page contains tasks under the heading 'Today'.
2. It wasn't clear that the user had to pick one of the interaction options on that screen.
3. The survey seems too long and cluttered to look at.
4. The trends can be better adapted to suit the activity.
