Personalizing & interacting with mood tracking data

Personal Emotions Tracker is a mobile mental health tracking app that offers a conversational mode to infer user's mental states as well as an avatar visualization of their mood.

TIMELINE

PROBLEM
Current Mood Tracking apps

The mood tracking apps in the market today lack in-depth tracking and reflecting capabilities. They also had a high input overhead on the user; and did not support long-term behavior change because the user doesn't have easy access to the data.



PROCESS
Design Thinking

I followed IDEO's Design Thinking method but I iterated on my initial screens after doing a round of testing.






EMPATHIZE
Literature Review

I surveyed apps on the play store and looked at academic papers to find opportunities for improvement and current research efforts in this domain. I found multiple possibilities and room for improvement as summarized below:


TYPES OF MOOD TRACKERS

Two types: Survey-based & Chatbot-based

However, they lack in-depth tracking, and reflecting capabilities.
AUTOMATED MOOD SENSING

Smartwatch sensors are able to detect heart-rate biosignals, sleep and activities.

This drastically reduces user input overhead.
MULTI-MODAL TRACKING & REFLECTING ON DATA

Multi-modal tracking, natural language feedback and contextual information are some of the ways to support long-term behavior change.

DEFINE
Storyboard

This storyboard captures how Zachary, our primary persona might interact with Personal Emotions Tracker in one specific scenario.



Based on the storyboard, I came with the following use cases that an ideal user would experience:
1. Logging post-sleep mood on the watch
2. Interacting with the chatbot during stressful situations
3. Interacting with just-in-time interventions based on bio-signals on the watch
4. Looking at past data and reflecting on their mood




IDEATE
App Features

The storyboard and the use cases gave me a clear idea about the app's features, which are summarized below:


MULTI-MODAL ENGAGEMENT

One-Touch (simply tap a smiley face to log mood)

Survey (answer a paginated questionnaire)

Chatbot (converse with the animal 'avatar')
EFFICIENT CUSTOMIZATION & REFLECTIONS

Avatar Customization (change the avatar, customize color palette)

Mood trends (time-based, linked to contextual data)

Easy-to-follow visualizations (bar charts, time series-graphs) to promote reflection
PERSONAL CONTEXT INTEGRATION

Automatic population of activity and event data from the iOS calendar app or Google Calendar
SMARTWATCH INTERFACE

Respond to just-in-time interventions and ask the chatbot a question using voice

RETROSPECTIVE
Reflection

I had the following learnings from this project:

1. Conducting literature review led to a stronger concept and well-thought out functionalities for the app.
2. Being my first time conducting user testing, I was able to realize its value and witnessed first-hand the impact it has on a product.
3. Designing for the apple watch compelled me to prioritize and showcase only the most essential elements on the interface.
4. More time and resources would've added a layer of customization, pertaining to the animal avatars and depicting user's real-time mood on the animal.
