Participatory design is a unique and compelling method to design solutions with (rather than for) an audience. And this project allowed me to do the same, and create something truly wonderful for the participant.

TIMELINE

PROCESS
Initial Interview to get insight into their everyday life

I began by conducting an open-ended interview with the participant to understand their experiences not only with technology but those around their everyday lives. I intended to discover mutliple pain points and use that as a starting point to begin ideating solutions.

These are the key themes that emerged from the interview:

Forgetting chores & everyday objects around the house
Unnecessary features, accidental sends, and small icons on smartphones
Recent tranisition from Apple to Andrioid




PROJECT FOCUS
FINDING EVERYDAY OBJECTS AROUND THE HOUSE

Based on the participant's preferences and most pertinent dilemma




BRAINSTORMING
Solution 1: Alexa Skill

Based on sticker-like tracker tags which when stuck on the desired object would keep track of it, the Alexa skill would prompt the location of this object whenever desired.




BRAINSTORMING
Solution 2: Phone-based App

Also based on the same tags, an app would guide you to the desired object and also use AR to locate it in real-time by simply scanning the camera around the room.


PROCESS
Co-designing the app with the participant and customizing it for them

I created a low-fidelity prototype to show the participant what is possible and get them into the mindset. And in no time, they started suggesting and even ideating whole features for the app. They provides some great insights and truly made the app what it is.
