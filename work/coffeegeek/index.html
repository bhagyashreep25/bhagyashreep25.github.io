PROCESS
Strategic Visual Design

I came up with a visual design strategy based on research and moodboarding, and then followed through with multiple design iterations.






RESEARCH
Why Change?

The content of the blog is well curated and one of a kind, however the current design steals focus away from the content. The interesting content caught my attention but it took a backseat due to the website's jarring and outdated interface.


INCONSISTENT USE OF FONTS
USE OF MULTIPLE BRIGHT & CONTRASTING COLORS
NO CLEAR GRID STRUCTURE OR CONTENT ORGANIZATION
ILLEGIBLE TEXT OVER ASSETS




Target Audience

The target audience for my redesign is the younger demographic of coffee brewers with varying levels of experience and diverse taste. These users are also at various stages on the coffee brewing journey which is reflected in their brewing equipment, process and preferences.



Design Strategy


LESS IS MORE

Take a step back, declutter and try not to do too many things at once.
EARTHY TONES

Let the browns of the coffee (from images) shine by pairing them with greens and neutrals.
WHITESPACE

Use whitespace to build hierarchy and make long articles easy to skim through.




IDEATE
Wireframes

As the current homepage was unorganized and way too long, I create some wireframes to figure out the layout that would best suit the website.
I balanced multiple aspects such as providing enough diverse content while highlighting key articles, and showcasing different categories of content while creating the layout of the home page.






VISUAL DESIGN
Branding and Identity

The current logo is not indicative of the brand's values. It does not appeal to my target audience. It also fails to make the most of the name 'CoffeeGeek', which is actually pretty cool.
I wanted to play on the brand name itself for the new logo. Glasses characterize a geek. Hence, I made glasses out of coffee beans, which plays right into the young, modern and fun aesthetic I was aiming for.

RETROSPECTIVE
Outcome

I submitted an interactive working prototype as a deliverable which achieved the following:

1. Made coffee content modern, aesthetic, and accessible.
2. Created a basic design system for desktop as well mobile made the design process efficient.



Reflection & Future Scope

I had the following learnings from this project:

1. Starting from a design strategy with a target audience in mind allowed me to keep my designs grounded and focused.

2. Refreshing the logo gave me a starting point for the entire visual identity.

3. I went through several iterations of wireframe layouts and high-fidelity screens for the home page which taught me the importance of not marrying to one idea and the value of experimenting.

4. Setting up a design system helped keep my designs organized and consistent. I also considered accessibility when it comes to contrast.

5. Because I started with the desktop designs first, they proved as a template when I progressed to the mobile version. I was able to prioritize what content is the most important and how to best create an experience for somebody accessing the website on mobile.

6. More time and resources would be needed to scale the design to the diverse content on the website including pictures.
