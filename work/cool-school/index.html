COOL SCHOOL
UX RESEARCH • VISUAL DESIGN • CO-DESIGN WITH KIDS
Reimagining & validating conflict resolution for kids

Cool School is an old kids’ game developed by veteran game designer FJ Lennon in collaboration with US Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service. It teaches them how to resolve conflicts in a healthy manner.

PROBLEM
Updating the UX & aesthetics of the game

I was tasked with envisioning how Cool School would look like in current day. The long term goal was to revamp not only the look and feel but also the experience of the game, by bringing its design and infrastructure up to date.



PROCESS
Working in Design Sprints

We worked in 5-week sprints each containing 5 stages: map, sketch, decide, prototype and test. Because our target audience was kids aged 5-7 years, we conducted our testing with KidsTeam, a group of kids at University of Maryland helping with research and designing technologies for kids.






SPRINT 1 GOAL
REPLAYABILITY

What makes the players motivated to play the game?




Design Ideas


OVERVIEW OF THE GAME

Create a prototype that contains sneak peek of the entire game with all the features of customization, gameplay, rewards, and challenges.

As our target audience is kids, I wanted to give them a complete experience so that its easier for them to treat it like a real game.
AVATAR CUSTOMIZATION

To give the game a fresh look, I created avatar assets from scratch that lended itself easily to a customization system easily.

I also opted for shapes as the basis for the avatar which do not have gender or race to build a more inclusive environment.
REWARDS & CHALLENGES

The Cool School rewards system was rebuilt to enhance replayability. Along with earning coins, the player can level up, look for daily challenges, unlock achievements and also buy items in the shop.

SPRINT 2 GOAL
CONTENT, AESTHETICS & FLUID GAMEPLAY

How can we use existing resources but still create a modern game?




Design Ideas


OLD CONTENT WITH MODERN ASSETS

I borrowed from the old game scripts and chose 4 conflicts to prototype. I made the decision to use the new avatar assets to also create the NPCs that will be bickering.
ANIMATION

To give the feel of characters actually talking, I created scenario animations that depict a conflict.

This will be a far more compelling experience for the kids because they can see the conflict unravel much like a movie.
MAP & MUSIC

In order to emulate finding a conflict in the school, I added a map that gives a sense of location and also finding NPCs and their conflicts.

I also added background music to further add to the experience and making it fun for the kids.
